TGV

The French TGV, introduced in 1981, quickly established itself as one of the world’s fastest and most efficient trains, connecting major French cities with unprecedented speed. Reaching speeds over 300 km/h, the TGV pioneered high-speed rail travel in Europe, setting multiple records and changing the way people traveled long distances. The sleek, aerodynamic design, paired with France’s commitment to innovation, made the TGV a global icon in train technology. It remains an essential part of European rail infrastructure, demonstrating the efficiency, sustainability, and convenience of high-speed rail networks.

Characteristics

  • Length: 250 meters
  • Speed: Up to 130 km/h
  • Year of Release: 2000
  • Seating Capacity: 1000 passengers
  • Route: Delhi to Mumbai
